Nairobi, Kenya

The green city in the sun, and safari capital of the world

East Africa's most cosmopolitan city, Nairobi is the beating heart of Kenya, an exciting, modern city that counterpoints the untrammelled natural beauty to be found elsewhere in the country.

The city had a population of over 4.3 million in the 2019 census, while the metropolitan area has a projected population in 2022 of 10.8 million. Nairobi is commonly referred to as The Green City in the Sun.

Nairobi was founded in 1899 by colonial authorities in British East Africa, as a rail depot on the Uganda - Kenya Railway. The town quickly grew to replace Mombasa as the capital of Kenya in 1907. After Kenya's independence in 1963, Nairobi became the capital of the Republic of Kenya.

During Kenya's colonial period, the city became a centre for the colony's coffee, tea and sisal industry. The city lies in the south central part of Kenya, at an elevation of 1,795 metres (5,889 ft).

Nairobi has the only national park in the world which borders a major city.

  • Nairobi National Park was established in 1946 about 7km south of the city.
  • The park is home to over 30 animal species including lions, giraffes & zebras.
  • Tourist interests include the wildebeest and zebra migrations in July and August, the Ivory Burning Site Monument, and the Nairobi Safari Walk.
  • The park's Wildlife Conservation Education Centre has lectures and video shows about wildlife and guided tours of the park and animal orphanage.

Interesting facts about Nairobi

  • The city originated in the late 1890s as a colonial railway settlement, taking its name from a water hole known to the Maasai people as Enkare Nairobi (“Cold Water”).
  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport, 9 miles (15 km) to the southwest, is one of the chief international airports in Africa.
  • Nairobi is home to several educational institutions, including the University of Nairobi, Kenyatta University College, Kenya Polytechnic University College, and Kenya Institute of Administration.
  • Nairobi has its own wildlife attractions, with a fabulous national park on its doorstep and several reserves dedicated to the wellbeing of elephants, giraffes, rhinos and more.
